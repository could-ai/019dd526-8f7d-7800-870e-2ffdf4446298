**NARRATOR (V.O.)**
*(Pacing: Slow, quiet, almost whispering... drawing the listener in)*
This is a story about the importance of truly knowing people. 
You may look at someone and think you understand them... think you know what goes on behind their eyes. 
But in reality? You have no idea what they’re thinking. 
Or worse... what they’re capable of.

*(Beat)*

This happened about four or five years ago. Life had been suffocating. The kind of stress that sits on your chest and refuses to leave. My girlfriend, Stacy, suggested we rent a cabin deep in the woods. Just to escape. To breathe.

I loved the idea, but... our bank accounts didn't. We needed to split the cost, so we invited my best friend, Randy, and his girlfriend. Since it was the dead of winter, Stacy found the perfect place: isolated, quiet, with a large, warm fireplace.

*(Pause. Subtle sound effect: A cold, distant winter wind)*

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**
*(Pacing picks up slightly, a hint of unease)*
A week before the trip, Randy and his girlfriend broke up. It was sudden. It was messy. 
Stacy and I debated returning his money, but the truth was, we couldn't afford the cabin without him. When I called Randy, he insisted we keep it. He wanted us to go.

But the very next day, he called back. He said he found someone else to bring. 
We weren’t comfortable taking a total stranger to a secluded cabin in the middle of nowhere. But Randy begged. He even offered to pay my full share of the trip. Against our better judgment... we agreed.

*(Beat)*

Her name was Lucy.

*(Audio: Music shifts to something slightly dissonant and uneasy)*

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**
When we met her, she seemed harmless. Quiet. Shy, even. During the long drive up into the mountains, she barely spoke a single word. She just stared out the window into the frozen, passing trees.

At first, the cabin was exactly what we needed. We settled in, lit a fire. Later that evening, we sat down to play a simple game of Uno. 

*(Tone becomes sharper, more intense)*
Stacy placed her second-to-last card and smiled. "Uno," she said.
Instantly... Lucy snapped.
She exploded with a terrifying, blinding anger. She screamed, accusing Stacy of cheating. But it wasn’t just a loud argument—she shrieked. Like a petulant, deranged child. It was a massive, unsettling overreaction to a meaningless card game.

*(Pause)*

By dinner, it was as if nothing had happened. Lucy acted completely normal. But the conversation took a dark turn. She began oversharing deeply personal, uncomfortable details about her and Randy. We all just sat there in stunned, awkward silence.

That night, the real nightmare began. 
Stacy shook me awake in the pitch black. Through the thin walls of the cabin, we could hear Randy and Lucy arguing. Low, venomous whispers. We tried to ignore it. 

*(Audio: Slow, muted heartbeat sound effect begins)*

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**
*(Voice drops, trembling slightly)*
The next night... things got worse.
I jolted awake to find Randy standing over my bed. He was pale. Terrified. Trembling.
He told me he had woken up to find Lucy standing at the foot of his bed... just watching him sleep in the dark.
When he pulled back the covers... he found a large kitchen knife hidden in the sheets.

*(Quick beat of silence. Heartbeat stops.)*

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**
Randy had slipped out of the room to find us. As he crept through the dark cabin, he saw her. 
Lucy was wandering the halls. Pacing. Humming a low, eerie tune to herself, almost like she was sleepwalking.

Then... the floor creaked beneath Randy’s foot.

*(Audio: A sharp, loud creak of wood)*

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**
She stopped dead in her tracks.
She didn't turn around. She just spoke to the empty dark.
"I see you."

*(Fast pacing now, urgent)*
Randy ran. He bolted into our room and locked the door. We sat in the dark, barely breathing, listening to her footsteps moving around outside our door. 
Then—**SLAM**. The heavy wooden front door shut. 
She had gone outside. Into the freezing, pitch-black forest. 
With the knife.

*(Audio: Wind howling effect, distant and cold)*

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**
We waited in terror. Hours passed. Eventually... she came back inside.
When morning finally broke, we cautiously walked out into the living room. Lucy was sitting there, drinking coffee. Acting completely, horrifyingly normal. Smiling like it was just another sunny day.

But as we packed to leave in a hurry, Randy saw it. Peeking out from the zipper of her overnight bag... was the handle of the large kitchen knife.

*(Audio: Music swells briefly, then drops to a low drone)*

**NARRATOR (V.O.)**
We left immediately. The drive back was dead silent.
The moment we returned, Randy cut all contact with her. We tried to look her up later. To find out who—or what—she really was.
But we found nothing. No social media. No records. 
It was as if she had never existed at all.

*(Final, chilling beat)*

You may think you know the people you let into your life.
But believe me... you never really know what’s waiting in the dark.
